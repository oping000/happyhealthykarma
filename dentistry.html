🦷 When should you see a dentist?

Regular dental care is one of the most important parts of overall health. Many people avoid the dentist due to cost or anxiety — but catching problems early saves money and pain in the long run.

Every 6 months

Routine cleaning and checkup — the foundation of good dental health.

Tooth pain

Don't wait — tooth pain rarely goes away on its own and can signal infection.

Bleeding gums

Could be early gum disease — a periodontist can help before it gets worse.

Broken tooth

See a dentist within 24 hours — a broken tooth can become infected quickly.

Wisdom teeth

Most people need wisdom teeth removed between ages 17-25.

Children

First dental visit by age 1 or when first tooth appears — pediatric dentists specialize in kids.

💡 Types of dental specialists

Not all dental concerns need the same type of provider. Here's a quick guide to dental specialists:

General Dentist

Cleanings, fillings, x-rays, basic extractions — your first stop for most dental needs.

Orthodontist

Braces, Invisalign, bite correction — specializes in straightening teeth.

Oral Surgeon

Wisdom teeth, implants, complex extractions, jaw surgery.

Periodontist

Gum disease, gum recession, deep cleaning treatments.

Endodontist

Root canals and infections inside the tooth.

Pediatric Dentist

Dental care specifically for children — trained to work with kids.
